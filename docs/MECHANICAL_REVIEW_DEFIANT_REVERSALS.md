# Arcane Backlash Mechanical Review

## Pack 2 of 4: Defiant Reversals

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Module | PF2E Critical Forge: Arcane Backlash |

| Cards Reviewed | 30 |
| Final Result | Passed after revision |
| Review Date | 2026-07-21 |

## Scope

This review covered schema validity, correct benefit ownership for the saving creature, PF2e timing and terminology, action economy, mechanical uniqueness, cross-module overlap, conditional consumption, and table usability.

## Initial Findings

The 0.2.2 draft required nine redesigns and nine targeted adjustments. The main clusters were repeated immediate Steps, fear recovery already used in Combat Momentum, duplicate cover reduction, caster-location mechanics already used in Miscast Repercussions, overloaded terrain exceptions, and ally-action mechanics duplicated from Martial Openings.

## Applied Redesigns

| Card | Revision |
|---|---|
| DR-001 Stand Through It | Replaced the immediate Step with an anchored defensive wake that improves cover against the original caster while the defender holds position. |
| DR-002 Not Today | Replaced frightened reduction with a short adjacent ally aura against magical fear and emotion effects. |
| DR-007 Seen It Now | Replaced the repeated-save penalty reduction and Step fallback with a reaction to Raise a Shield or Take Cover against a repeated spell. |
| DR-011 Feedback Glare | Replaced cover reduction with magical direction tracing and a one-use Seek bonus. |
| DR-014 Resonance Returned | Replaced persistent caster-location revelation with a flare that identifies the next Sustained spell effect during the Sustain action. |
| DR-019 Reclaim the Ground | Replaced stacked terrain, cover, concealment, and entry exceptions with a single allied reality anchor against forced movement or relocation. |
| DR-024 Opening in the Pattern | Replaced generic reaction suppression with the ability for one ally to ignore a spell-created AC or save bonus. |
| DR-025 Your Turn | Replaced generic Seek, Recall Knowledge, and Interact choices with borrowing the defender's higher modifier for the same save type. |
| DR-029 Unimpressed by the Cosmos | Replaced free Demoralize with a choice for the caster to spend an action recomposing or publicly telegraph the next spell. |

## Applied Adjustments

- DR-005 now shortens round-based effects without ever extending them.
- DR-008 now targets a spell or magical effect for counteract checks.
- DR-009 recognizes the caster's true space without overriding explicit random-target or decoy procedures.
- DR-013 now defines its timing after spell, space, targets, and area are chosen but before rolls.
- DR-018 uses the official dangerous-terrain term and limits entry protection to the triggering spell.
- DR-020 now defines qualifying effects by cover or difficult-terrain mechanics and uses delayed conditional application instead of a Step fallback.
- DR-021 is correctly classified as Strong.
- DR-022 no longer adds a post-resolution Step.
- DR-030 requires the saving creature to perceive the caster beginning the secret or deceptive action.

## Final Distribution

| Impact | Cards |
|---|---:|
| Light | 8 |
| Moderate | 16 |
| Strong | 6 |

All 30 cards remain manual. Their effects depend on the original caster, specific spell identity, reactions, selected allies, ongoing magical effects, geometry, or conditional consumption.

## Final Result

**Mechanical Review: Passed after revision.**

Defiant Reversals now has a clear identity: a critical save exposes a magical flaw and converts resistance into information, shared defense, reclaimed space, or a brief reversal without reflecting the original spell or stealing resources.
